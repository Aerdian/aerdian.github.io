The Influence of Perceptions on Religious Discrimination

A research study conducted at Lake Forest College, which suggests that a lack of understanding is to blame for religious discrimination, and until knowledge can be increased universally, a lack of acceptance toward those of a variety of faiths is expected to continue. This research has paved the way for some interesting new potential questions to consider, such as figuring out how much students already understand different religions and how this impacts their perceptions.

linguistic anthropology

Translanguaging Beyond the Classroom: Language, Education, and Social Change in Africa

The struggle against epistemicide in post-colonial contexts continue to center linguistic considerations in search of decoloniality and de-westernization through contextually relevant and culturally sustaining knowledge ecologies (Ndlovu-Gatsheni, 2018; Mignolo, 2012; Nyamnjoh, 2012). While the debates about the trajectories of what Mignolo calls “pluriversality”—in contradistinction with “universality”—unfurls, the need for advocacy for linguistic diversity in education remain critical. To extend beyond popular advocacy practices that prioritize academic and state actors, this project creates vlogs as digital advocacy tools directed at African urbanites and analyzes conversations they generate to explore new frontiers for bridging innovations in applied linguistics and the prospects for decolonial futures.
